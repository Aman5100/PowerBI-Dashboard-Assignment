OBJECTIVE:-

The goal of this project is to analyze airline performance data and visualize key operational metrics. It seeks to uncover trends in revenue, passenger traffic, delays, and profitability across different routes and regions, helping stakeholders identify strengths and bottlenecks.

INSIGHTS:-

1)Revenue Trends: Monthly revenue shows strong variation, with January outperforming February. Certain routes like DFW–SEA and LAX–SYD consistently generate high revenue.

2)Route Profitability: Profitability is uneven—while DFW–SEA and NYC–CHI routes deliver strong margins, some international routes (e.g., NYC–DXB) show high delays impacting efficiency.

3)Delay Analysis: Average delays are higher on international flights compared to domestic ones, with some routes exceeding 150 minutes. This highlights operational challenges in long-haul sectors.

4)Passenger Load: High passenger volumes are observed on routes like NYC–LAX and BOS–LHR, indicating strong demand corridors.

5)Operational Balance: The dashboard reveals trade-offs—routes with high revenue sometimes suffer from longer delays, suggesting areas for optimization.

CONCLUSION:-
we gained a clear view of airline performance across multiple dimensions. It enables data-driven decision-making, highlights profitable routes, identifies delay-prone sectors, and provides actionable insights for improving efficiency and customer satisfaction.
